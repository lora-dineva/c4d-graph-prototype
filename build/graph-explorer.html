N-tier Supplier Monitor — Graph Explorer

This is the supply chain your spreadsheets can't see. Most supplier systems list who you buy from directly — your Tier-1s. They go quiet about who supplies them, and who supplies them. That is exactly where the disruptions of the last five years started: a distressed chip maker two tiers down, a “backup” supplier that shares one factory with your primary, a “European” source quietly controlled from the region you were trying to leave.

The Graph Explorer joins your part, plant, programme, ownership and risk data into one connected map, so a risk landing anywhere in the chain can be followed all the way to the cars and plants it threatens — in seconds, not a six-week manual study. Pick a scenario below to be walked through a real example, or take a quick tour of the workspace first.

How to read the map

Each point is one thing in the chain — a supplier, a site, a part, an assembly, a plant or a vehicle programme. Its shape tells you what type it is (see the key, bottom-left). Its border colour tells you how exposed it is: red critical, orange high, amber medium, blue low, green a genuinely healthy path. Lines are real dependencies — what flows into what. A double ring marks a single point of failure: a part with no alternative route.

Click anything to inspect it on the right. Use Trace impact on a supplier or part to light up everything downstream it would take with it. The three scenarios below show the patterns a flat list can never reveal.
